item: H-BAJ-2
date: 2026-04-15T00:00:00Z
summary: Pin a regression vector in test/broker-hash.test.ts and carry two H-BAJ-3 flags.
context: |
  Work item spec required stability/divergence/length/charset/invalid-secret tests,
  but every stability assertion compared digest-to-digest, so a subtle change
  (base64url -> base64, different key slicing, different truncation length) would
  shift both halves and pass silently. Reviewer flagged this plus two H-BAJ-3
  concerns: (a) committed broker_secret in config.json is world-readable in public
  repos, (b) narrow TOCTOU race between concurrent first-run nw invocations
  generating distinct project_ids.
decision: |
  1. Added a pinned-vector test: makeBrokerHasher(all-zero 32-byte secret)("H-BAJ-2")
     must equal "iQ_0ZkX1oVsd1md_gOjogY". JSDoc includes the one-liner to
     regenerate if the algorithm ever changes intentionally.
  2. Deferred reviewer's two H-BAJ-3 concerns (config.local.json override docs,
     idempotent identity registration on the broker) to H-BAJ-3. Neither is in
     scope for a pure helper module with no call sites.
rationale: |
  The pinned vector is cheap (one line) and catches a whole class of silent
  regressions that the spec'd tests miss. Expanding scope into broker protocol
  or config docs would violate the "pure module, no call sites" boundary in the
  work item; better to surface the signal to the H-BAJ-3 implementer than to
  quietly absorb it here.
